  • Numerical Method of solving calculus of variation using gradient descent
    Calculus of variation is a technique used in various fields of mathematics and mechanics. One known method of solving an optimization problem of such is through an Euler-Lagrange equation. However, in complex systems, solving Euler-Lagrange equation can be very difficult. Therefore, I introduce you a numerical method of solving such optimization problem using gradient descent and fourier expansion. You can download the package from a github repository.

  • Adaptive Optics Simulator
    We say that the stars are "twinkling" because their images are distorted by the atmosphere. It is romantic in some sense, but when it comes to an observation, abberation of image is a big trouble. Adaptive optics is a technology of reconstructing images that are distorted by a turbulence in Earth’s atmosphere. I created a simulation that duplicates the process of adaptive optics and simulated a distortion of image by an atmosphere and the reconstruction of it by an adaptive process.
    Explore More
  • An analysis of a chain falling faster by collision
    Galileo Galilei broke an old misconception that the heavy objects fall faster. However, if a ladder-like chain is falling on the floor, surprisingly, it turns out that the chain falls faster than the identical chain falling in the air. The collision of chain and the floor keeps accelerating the chain, and therefore, it falls faster. I created a simulation of the phenomenon and showed how fast the chain falls when colliding with the floor.
